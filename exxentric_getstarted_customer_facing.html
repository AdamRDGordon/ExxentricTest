First training habit

Your first program

Start with a training block that is simple enough to complete. Choose microdosing for consistency, or a 2-day split if you want slightly more volume.

Low friction

Microdosing

One set of a few key exercises, 5–7 days per week, taking less than 10 minutes. Ideal if you are starting from scratch or building the habit of frequent flywheel exposure.

Best forHabit building, beginners, busy schedules
Frequency5–7 short sessions per week
GoalConsistency before complexity
More structure

2-day split

Alternate two sessions over the week, adding a little more volume per muscle group while keeping the plan easy to understand and repeat.

Day 1Quads, calves, chest, shoulders
Day 2Hamstrings, back, arms, core
Frequency2–4 sessions per week
Keep your device fresh

Maintenance and safety checkpoints

Once you have started using your device regularly, these simple checkpoints help keep training safe and your equipment in good condition.

AreaDoDon’t
Training techniqueControl the eccentric portion.Never slam the pulley block into the device.
Drive beltStop and untangle a tangled drive belt immediately.Never train with a tangled belt.
ExitingCome to a full stop before stepping off or disconnecting.Do not disconnect before the movement has stopped.
MaintenanceTrim the drive belt at the first signs of wear.Do not wait until the belt breaks.
StorageUnwind and roll up the drive belt tightly after training.Do not leave it loosely stored or partially wound.
